Instructor Insights & Discussion

Thompson Sampling and UCB performed best. Why? Because they are adaptive—they balance exploration and exploitation dynamically based on uncertainty, reducing budget waste on subpar arms.
A/B Testing. It forces a set budget (e.g., $2,000) into exploration regardless of early performance results, spending equal money on the 0.5 arm as the 0.8 arm.
Traditional A/B testing is static. It separates the exploration phase from the decision phase. Once the experiment starts, the allocation is fixed until the end, ignoring real-time feedback that could save budget.
  • Ads System: Thompson Sampling. It handles high-velocity data and multiple arms efficiently.
  • Clinical Trial: UCB (Upper Confidence Bound). It provides more mathematically rigorous bounds on regret, which is crucial when human health is involved.
With a smaller budget, A/B testing suffers the most because it might not reach statistical significance. Adaptive methods like Thompson Sampling excel because they prioritize the best arms almost immediately.
Regret increases for all methods as it becomes harder to differentiate the 'best' arm. This requires much more exploration (budget) to reach a confident decision.
